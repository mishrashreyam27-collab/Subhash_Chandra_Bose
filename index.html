Early Life

Born on January 23, 1897, in Cuttack, Odisha, into a well-off Bengali family. He was deeply influenced by Swami Vivekananda's teachings and showed early signs of patriotism and leadership.

Political Rise

Joined the Indian National Congress and was elected its President in 1938 and 1939. He had ideological differences with Mahatma Gandhi and eventually formed the Forward Bloc.

Formation of INA

Escaped from British surveillance in 1941, reached Germany, and then Japan. He led the Indian National Army (INA) to fight the British with the slogan "Give me blood, and I shall give you freedom!"

Mysterious Death

Reportedly died in a plane crash on August 18, 1945, in Taiwan. His death remains a mystery and subject of speculation and investigation.
